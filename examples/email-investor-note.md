> **Why the dual-version pattern matters for email of this kind.** Three reasons. (1) For the writer, drafting in long-form prose first and then compressing to HumanCode is a sense-check on whether the structural argument actually survives — every operational claim, every conditional, every commitment has to either fit into HC or be explicitly left out. The HC version often surfaces decisions the prose was hedging around. (2) For an AI loading the email — the recipient's inbox-triage agent, an investor's CRM that auto-extracts deal terms, a personal summariser — the `.hc.md` companion is a fraction of the token cost of the prose, and the structural fields (`FundingTracks: CautiousMe vs VisionaryMe`, `Ask: Help_FindSweetSpot`) are immediately machine-actionable. (3) For the human recipient, the two versions answer different needs. A reader who engages with the prose gets the warmth, the vulnerability, the rhythm of someone thinking out loud about their ambition; a time-poor reader (a busy investor scanning thirty pitches at midnight) gets the forty-line summary and can decide in two minutes whether to engage further. The dual-version pattern means a single email can serve both readers without choosing between them — and crucially, the long-form version is preserved exactly as the founder wrote it, not flattened into "investor speak."

**Subject:** Competitor reference (Wecasa) + something candid about the vision

**To:** Zak

Hi Zak,

Just thought I'd mention one of the few competitors I've found in the space for what we're trying to achieve. i.e. not all the small cleaning agencies or companies, but more like the vision we've been discussing.

Wecasa, French company, founded in 2016, backed by VC and valued at $70–100M. They offer Cleaning, Housekeeping, Hairdressing and Massage.

- https://www.wecasa.co.uk
- https://app.dealroom.co/investors/ixo_private_equity
- https://app.dealroom.co/companies/wecasa

Built pre-AI, it's an app-based booking system that functions much like our current one does. Presumably going for a WeWork association in the brand, though questionable how advisable that would be given how that went!

Over 200 employees in their office, so by building AI-Native we stand to be able to move much quicker and cheaper into new areas, plus offer a better experience and pay for cleaners and customers at much cheaper cost. i.e. I'm confident we can compete with them, maybe one day acquire them or sell to them.

But it is an example of the potential of the market, and that we're not the only ones innovating in the home service space. This may help with investor perceptions?

They will have to unbuild their system to integrate AI, and while they may have the cash to do it, they might not have the stomach to make their staff redundant! Which means they will likely be slow to update.

As an aside, I have a personal life goal of having a net worth in the trillions by the time I die — I don't think I've mentioned that yet — that is, if money even exists in the same form by then (crypto / creator economy / who knows!). For me, it's not about the money, it's entirely about knowing how much of an impact I've made — how much value I've added to the world.

£100M in revenue in under 2 years is my secret personal target for ShineOn. I know it will take longer in reality, but as you're familiar with 4HWW you'll know the strategy of aim high and apply Parkinson's Law. I like to think as big as I can stomach and then work backwards from there.

If I'd been aiming for a personal income of £10k/m and a part-time lifestyle business, I'd probably have achieved that by now with Shine On Cleans. It's because my sights are set on £100M in 2yrs and £1B in 5yrs that I've prioritised my time in the ways I have.

The truth about my previous businesses is that I never believed any of them could be large enough to fuel the big-picture goals I have. £50M is my f**k you money number, and I will build companies around all my passions to turn that into £1T. Until I hit that figure, I'm focusing on the enterprise that is most likely to get me to that first £50M.

I struggle to speak about the true scope of my vision because most people around me look at me like I'm crazy, which weakens my resolve, so it's safer to keep it to myself. I don't care about status, except for how it affects my ability to serve the people who need what I have to give. That was the problem in the coaching business — everyone told me that to get to the next level I had to project wealth to convince customers to listen, and that rubbed me the wrong way.

But I will ask for your help in finding the sweet spot between boldly ambitious, and unrealistically foolish, when presenting my story and the business' goals to the investors, which I'm sure we'll get to in time.

Wecasa shows the overlooked potential in the home services sector, and that tech-powered solutions in the space can grow into impressive operations.

But what they've done in 9 years, I want to do in 2.

The cautious parts of me think that raising £250k–£500k first and "showing what we can do with it" is the sensible way to play it.

The visionary in me wants to be bold and aim for £5M so we can move 10× faster, build something incredible and bulletproof, and most importantly: impact tens of thousands of cleaners, groomers, gardeners and more, while also helping millions of regular people to benefit from the same mindset that's served me so well: delegate everything you don't enjoy, focus on your strengths and let the pros help!

I'd use the money to hire the best in the AI space we can find, as well as fractional executives to help train and build the AI Executive Agents, purchase existing cleaning operations at scale and promote like crazy.

I envision a <20 person Human Exec Board that simply oversees the agents and mostly are part-time / semi-retired, at the helm of a billion-pound system that helps millions of people. By being so lean in the office, we can dominate the marketing and win the hearts and minds of the nation/world as a leader in real-world AI support services. We can also go into developing countries fast, and possibly produce more change for local communities than decades of aid has been able to.

I'll leave it there for now. Hope you have a great weekend and looking forward to our next steps!

David Caldicott
Co-Founder & Chief Support Officer
Shine On Cleans
